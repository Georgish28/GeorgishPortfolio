Most recent work

Itinerary Atlas

Duration: 10 Weeks

Problem: With the drop in Covid-19 restrictions there is a huge surge in travel, but what if you never traveled, new to travelling, or an experienced traveler but need ideas? Some itinerary planners assume you already know everything you want in your trip, whilst travel forums may be inconsistent in quality and validity with their feedback and recommendations.

Solution: Our solution to all of these issues is Itinerary Atlas! Itinerary Atlas is a platform where you can find and make itineraries all in one place.

Details

Competitive Analysis

Before further ideation, we analyzed our competitors and their services. We identified Wanderlog and online forums as our primary competitors. We examined their strengths and weaknesses. Wanderlog lacks itinerary guidance and primarily serves as a repository for known itineraries. Online forums offer itinerary ideas and feedback but lack user-friendly search functionality. Our findings confirmed that Itinerary Atlas would be a product that would bring something new into our target product space.

Details

Interview

To get further insight on how to move forward with Itinerary Atlas, we also wanted user feedback. Our group did so by conducting interviews with people we personally knew who traveled. Having interviews after our competitive analysis helped us further hone in on what aspects of our product we needed to work on.

Details

Sketches

After having a finalized understanding of what users desire and what our competitors lack. We began sketches for Itinerary Atlas. We aimed for a communal aspect in our design and because it was an early-stage design, we wanted to lay the groundwork for our prototypes and have all the essential elements present. All sketches provided are credited to Brendan Grant from our group.

Lo-Fi Prototype

After finalizing the sketches we discussed what aspects we liked and disliked about them. After discussing we went straight into Lo-Fi prototyping and made a combination of ideas we all liked. We just wanted to encompass all the necessities in it and not worry too much about the style yet. The prototype pictured is credited to Christian Martinez from our group.

Hi-Fi Prototype

For our finalized Hi-Fi prototype we added a color theme that we liked and started making it interactive. We aimed to have a clear accessible scheme and we stayed true to our initial prototype.

Reflection

This was my first time working on a long-term group project and I learned many new things, some of which were expected and others unexpected. Not being able to choose our own groups definitely made it a little more difficult at the beginning because the group needed to get acclimated with each other before starting. Conflicting schedules also made it very difficult to reach deadlines with a product that was presentable. I can say my skills in prioritizing work have improved due to this. Other skills that improved were my use of Figma and google teams products, which fell under my expectations.
